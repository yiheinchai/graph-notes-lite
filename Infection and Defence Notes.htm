�        Interferon

o   Background

�  Problem

�        Viruses enter the cells and hence are protected from the complement system

�        Interferon aims to bridge the gap

�  Solution

�        Antiviral proteins in the cell (restriction factors) restrict the ability of viruses to divide

o   Definition

�  Interferon � proteins that interferes with the replication of viruses.

o   Mechanism

�  Virus enters the cell cytoplasm and triggers the cell innate recognition receptors

�  This causes the cell to produce interferons.

�  Interferons spreads to the neighboring cells

�  With the presence of interferons, 1000 times more viruses are required to replicate within the cells. However, the viral resistant state prevents the cell from performing many of the normal cellular activities

�  This enables neighboring cells to become resistant to viral infection

�        Inflammation

o   Process

�  The immune system overreacts to a particular challenge

�  This overreaction results to the symptoms of the disease

o   Classical signs of inflammation

�  Cator (heat)

�  Rubor (redness)

�  Tumor (swelling)

�  Dolor (pain)

�  Function Laesa (loss of function)

o   Mechanism of Inflammation

�  Infection results in the recruitment of cells (neutrophils) and serum which contains proteins required for the complement system

�  To aid in recruitment, vasodilation occurs, enabling blood vessels to become bigger and hence increase the number of cells that can travel to infection site. Vasodilation results in redness in the skin.

�  To aid in recruitment, vasopermeability is increased, enabling the blood vessels to become leaky to that the components like neutrophils

�  Neutrophils that arrive will release toxic mediators to kill the pathogen. Mediators results in the damage of surrounding cells and damages the neighboring nerve endings. This causing tissue necrosis pain.

�  Due to the number of neutrophils that flood the infection site, the middle of the infection site runs out of oxygen and turns into a hypoxic state. This leads to the inability of tissue to function and hence tissue death.
